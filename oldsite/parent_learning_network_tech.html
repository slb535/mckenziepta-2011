Technology Night:
21st Century Learning in Action!

Wednesday, April 27
6:15pm - 8:30pm
McKenzie School

6:15pm - Sign in
6:30pm - Welcome (Auditorium)
6:45pm - First Session
7:40pm - Second Session

Registration has closed and is required as seating is limited.

Please contact Julie Johnson (jujohnson007@sbcglobal.net) if you have any questions about this upcoming event

click here to download the flyer to this event

Welcome!

The Parent Learning Network (PLN) is excited to offer McKenzie parents, along with their students, an interactive evening of learning, taught by our very own McKenzie staff.  Given technology is a cornerstone of 21st century learning and the PTA has generously gifted several forms of technology to the school (most notably our Smartboards and document cameras), PLN welcomes the opportunity to provide a forum for your family to experience 21st Century Learning together.  We hope you can come join the fun!

Below you will find several mini-course offerings.  Each parent and child team is invited to attend two learning sessions during the evening.  Advanced registration is required as seating is limited.  After reading each of the course descriptions, please choose three choices that you and your child would like to attend together (in order of preference) and we will do our best to accommodate your top picks.  Because we recognize the inherent value of parents learning alongside their children, this evening’s venue is intended for family teams. A “team” might consist of one parent and one child, two parents and one child, or one parent and possibly two children. Please indicate how many people are in your family’s team when signing up for a session to help us plan for needed equipment.

Doors will open at 6:15 pm. When you arrive in the lobby, you will be given a name badge that indicates the sessions in which you and your child (current McKenzie students only, please) are enrolled.  After a brief orientation in the Auditorium, the first 45-minute class will begin.  During the session, teachers will demonstrate a brief lesson on the stated technology topic, then provide participants with a hands-on activity, allowing each team to work together to apply a new skill. At the end of session one, there will be a brief passing period before the second session begins. The evening will wrap up by 8:30 pm.

Once all sessions have filled, a waiting list will be established. We will do our best to accommodate as many interested families as possible. This no-cost, PTA-sponsored event, is open to McKenzie families only. Don’t delay in signing up, as registration closes on April 22nd.

We look forward to sharing in a fun-filled evening of 21st Century Learning with you!

Best regards,

Julie Johnson

Course Offerings

Contemporary Curriculum: Learning in the 21st Century
Join McKenzie teachers, Courtney Lauer and Laura Wooten, in an exploration of the latest and greatest interactive tools used to engage all learners! In this session you will experience, first hand, what it’s like to be a 21st century student. This will include SMART Board activities and using the handheld Senteo remotes.

Podcasting: Create a McKenzie Virtual Tour
Podcasts can reach a global audience, and their creation fosters the development of essential 21st century skills of writing, speaking and listening. Join librarian, Sally Schoenberg, and technology teacher, Julie Garry, to learn how you can record your voice and insert a picture to create your own one-time podcast. During this session parent and child will create a podcast about a place in McKenzie School (library, music room, office, computer lab...).  Completed podcasts will be uploaded to our school website to create a virtual tour of our school!

SMART NOTEBOOK: An Alternative to Power Point
Teachers, Shelly Martin and Jessica Torf, will use SMART NOTEBOOK software to craft visually dynamic lessons and construct exciting interactive presentations. Students at McKenzie are now using SMART software instead of Power Point or Keynote to demonstrate their learning at a whole new level. During this session, parents and students will use many of the SMART NOTEBOOK tools to create a dynamic, 21st century multimedia product.

i Pod, i Touch, i Pad...Oh My!
Families learn about and interact with multiple educational applications which they can download to use on their iPod, iTouch or iPad. Join second grade teachers, Erin Schroeder and Lauren Siegel, as they demonstrate applications that are used in the classroom focusing on literacy, math and study skills. Parents and children will use iPods to practice skills and leave with a list of applications to delve into at home!

Dive into the World of Blogging!
Blogging can be a very useful way for parents and children to share their thoughts and feelings with others. Blogs can be used to create classroom connections, book clubs, journals and memoirs of specific experiences such as vacations and summer activities!  Join McKenzie teachers, Lynsee Whitman and Danielle Kalb, as they demonstrate how to create a blog. During this session, parents and students will create a personalized blog of their own.

Reading Rocks with Raz-Kids!
Using a laptop computer, families will dive into the world of reading using the interactive website, Raz-Kids. This website provides young readers with a variety of texts at their independent reading level. Join first grade teachers, Jane Dolkart and Kat Aten, as they demonstrate how Raz-Kids motivates students, gives independent practice, and assesses the student for teacher review. During this session, children will make a reading selection and take a comprehension quiz while teachers demonstrate the website and highlight key features that enhance instruction. This is a K-2 session open to students who already have a Raz password.
