Weekly Referendum Facts

If the referendum passes, District 39 will continue to be highly efficient and will still have the lowest operating cost per pupil compared to:

  • All other New Trier sender districts
  • Larger, comparably high-performing districts in Glenview, Highland Park, Hinsdale and Park Ridge

What do District 39’s operating numbers look like compared to the other New Trier sender districts?

  • D39 operating costs per pupil are the lowest of the six New Trier sender districts at $13,060 and rank 13th of 15 nearby and comparable school districts.
  • D39 operating costs per pupil are also lower than other high-performing districts with MORE students: Highland Park, Glenview and Hinsdale.
  • Wilmette’s tax rate generates the lowest property tax revenue per pupil in the region – 15th out of 15. If the referendum passes, Wilmette would move to 14th place.

How will education for our students compare to those in other New Trier sender districts if the referendum fails?

  • Reading intervention services are offered in all other sender districts (Winnetka, Kenilworth, Sunset Ridge, Avoca) for K-2 grade levels: here, there will be none.
  • At Winnetka, Kenilworth & Sunset Ridge, a science curriculum update has taken place within the past five years, but here (grades 6-8) the science curriculum is older than the students.
  • All other sender districts have foreign language instruction for 1-8 grade levels: here, none.
  • All other sender districts have certified librarians, at minimum, for K-4 grade levels: here, none K-8.
  • Avoca, Glencoe, Kenilworth & Winnetka all have technology infusion in their K-8 grade classrooms: here, no specialists available.
  • Instrumental music instruction begins in grade 5 at Winnetka & Glencoe, grade 4 at Kenilworth & Sunset Ridge, and grade 1 at Avoca: here, none.
