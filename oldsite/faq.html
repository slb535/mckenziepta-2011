Frequently Asked Questions

Helpful Hints from Parents

• Attend the PTA meetings. It’s a great way to get to know other parents and be in the know on school happenings!

• Be sure to bookmark www.mckenziepta.com on your computers and use the online calendar often when looking for event information.

• If you work outside the home be sure to look into activities that go on after hours that you can participate in such as the Ice Cream Social, which is on a Sunday afternoon, and Fall Frenzy, which is on a Friday night, and the Variety Show, which rehearses at night.

• Volunteer, volunteer! This is a great community of parents and children. You should get to know as many as you can.

• Be sure to purchase a directory at the opening day coffee and consider getting one for your office and car as well. Some families get one for each floor of the house!

• Hang around on the playground the first day of school and check out the volunteer opportunities.

• Check out the websites now and then to keep up to date with what is going on at McKenzie www.mckenziepta.com, www.wilmette39.org

• Check your child’s backpack EVERY DAY! There is important Backpack Express and other info you don’t want to miss so be sure to READ everything completely.

• Watch for the packet that comes home the first day of school. There is information that you need to return the next day.

• If you have misplaced a flyer, check the flyer archive and committee page. If you can’t find what you want on the website, just e-mail “askcheetah@mckenziepta.com” This inquiry address is located under the Contact Us tab on the PTA website.

• Be sure to check the menu if your child buys lunch. There may be a day he/she would prefer you make a lunch instead!

• Sign up to serve lunch on occasion. It’s a great way to put the faces with the names of the children your child is in class with.

• Order school supplies at the end of the year for the next school year. It saves time and insures your child has everything they need.

• Watch for yearbook order forms in the fall.

• McClubs are after school programs sponsored by the PTA and a ton of fun for the kids!

• An extra set of clothing is always wise to have in their lockers, especially during the wet and cold winter months.

• Enjoy your time here, it goes by too quickly!

McKenzie & District Terms

Butterfly Garden: The garden is located along the west wall of the school building, just south of the blacktop area. It is maintained by the landscape committee, who can always use help watering and weeding. Many classes integrate the garden into their curricula.

Citizenship Handbook: Each year the students and parents are asked to read this handbook about the expectations of school behavior and sign it.

Climbing Wall: A 8' x 30' designed-for-climbing “wall” located in the Small Gym. Foot and hand mounts as well as ball and hoop holds assist and challenge children in the exercise of vertical and horizontal climbing.

Connect-Ed: The automated emergency phone system for District 39. (formerly Pace 39).

Curriculum Differentiation: This is a process that a teacher uses in the classroom to help address learning differences between all children. In differentiating curriculum, teachers may modify the content (what students learn and at what rate they learn it), vary the thinking process, (how students will examine the ideas being taught), and/or assigning a range of products (students’ demonstration of their learning). Learning Behavior Specialists and a Curriculum Differentiation Support Teacher assist classroom teachers.

Enrichment Program: The enrichment program includes all efforts to enhance the educational curriculum. These efforts include field trips as well as music, science, cultural and literary programs.

Family Awareness Network (FAN): A parent-based, New Trier-based organization that provides lecture programs and information on parenting and social issues.

Habitat Garden: The garden is located at the Kindergarten entry. It was the Eagle Scout project for a McKenzie alum. Students study birds that visit the garden.

Homework Handbook: A guideline that was put together after a survey was conducted regarding homework.

Learning Behavioral Specialists (LBS): They assist in the classroom with Curriculum Differentiation.

Library Media Center (LMC): Another name for library.

Mikaelian Education Center (MEC): The operations of District 39, including the superintendent and administrators. The Village-Wide PTO meets there monthly. There is a copy center for the use of district PTAs and PTOs. It is located at 615 Locust Road in Wilmette. The phone number is (847) 256-2450.

McKenzie Online Interactive Website: Our website is www.mckenziepta.com

McKenzie Monitor: The brief, weekly update of upcoming events and lunch menu emailed to you weekly.

Parapro: An assistant teacher who is usually assigned to a classroom to meet the needs of one or more specific children but also helps the classroom as a whole.

PRAIRIE GARDEN: The garden located at the southwest corner of the school building. It was a gift from the PTA intended not only to beautify the school, but also help the students who are learning about the Midwest region.

Reading Deck: The reading deck is located in the northwest area of the playground and was renovated in 2003. When the weather is warm, teachers will sometimes conduct classes on the deck.

Reading Rally: “Reading Rally” is a group of books recommended for 4th graders by District 39. It can be found at the Wilmette Library and the Library Media Center.

Red Dot System: This is a system set up for all the correspondence that is sent home from the school. When an item is being sent home to all children, in the cases where siblings attend McKenzie, the family will receive only one copy to cut down on paper waste.

Safe Phone: Each day a child is absent, a parent must call the Safe Phone to report the absence. The McKenzie Safe Phone number is (847) 256-0369.

School Supply List: A list of items needed for the school year. You can use the list to buy your own supplies. For your convenience, these items can be pre-ordered at the end of the school year for the next year and will be delivered to your child’s classroom on the first day of school by the PTA.

Smartboard: a name-brand for a type of interactive whiteboard that is a large, touch controlled screen that works with a projector and computer. The projector throws the computer’s desktop image onto the interactive whiteboard, which acts as both a monitor and an input device. Users can write on the interactive whiteboard in digital ink or use a finger to control computer apps by pointing, clicking and dragging just as with a desktop mouse. The screens are mounted onto the walls and the projectors are mounted onto the ceilings in the classrooms.
