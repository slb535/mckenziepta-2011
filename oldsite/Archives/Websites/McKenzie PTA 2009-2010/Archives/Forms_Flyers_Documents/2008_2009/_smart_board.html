The McKenzie PTA is pleased to announce the purchase of LCDs and interactive white boards for all of the classrooms at McKenzie School.

Special thanks go to the Dronen Family for generously donating this technology for one classroom and District 39 for committing to match the same installments in two additional classrooms.   These donations as well as the 2008-2009 class gift of an LCD projector in the Art Room helped achieve our goal of equipping every classroom at McKenzie School!

Below are some answers to many frequently asked questions about this exciting project and technology.

What are LCD Projectors?

An LCD projector is a video projector for displaying video, images or computer data on a screen or any surface.  It is the modern version of the slide projector or overhead projector.  It is typically installed on the ceiling of a classroom.  In conjunction with an interactive white board, it can project a virtual chalkboard, beam an interactive globe and deliver interactive curriculum.

What are interactive white boards?

An interactive white board is a touch-sensitive display board that connects to a computer and digital projector to show computer images.  Computer applications can be controlled directly from the interactive white board and notes can be written on the board in digital ink and saved to share or review later.  Interactive white boards exponentially increase the capabilities of an LCD projector.

How will McKenzie students and teachers use this technology?

Interactive white boards combined with LCD projectors allow teachers to illustrate curriculum information visually, reinforce English vocabulary, and provide students with a variety of visual media.  Teachers and students jointly access and display websites, run educational software, run live video from a camera, record, capture, highlight and review notes from a classroom discussion and more.  One teacher from Kress, Texas reported, “It has enabled me to move from the sage on the stage to the guide on the side. Kids are in charge of the lessons; they are at the interactive whiteboard actively constructing their knowledge.” 

How was the PTA able to fund the purchase of interactive white boards and LCD projectors?

The primary source of funding is a result of restructuring the PTA Grocery Dollar program.  The changes in billing processes for the Grocery Dollar program freed approximately $70,000 this fiscal year. 

In the former Grocery Dollar process, McKenzie purchased all grocery dollars and collected the funds approximately twenty days later.   Beginning this year, patrons are paying for grocery dollars prior to the PTA purchasing them, thereby freeing these reserve dollars to be used more effectively.

The secondary source of funding is a result of a careful evaluation of PTA reserve funds.  An official “reserve target” was established this fiscal year.  The Executive Board adopted the Illinois PTA Association recommendation of 50% of the annual operating budget.   This resulted in an additional $30,000 to put toward the technology initiative.

Why should we spend these funds this year?

Per the National PTA guidelines, “As a rule, reserves should not exceed one-half of a PTA's budget for an average year.  If it does, the indication is that the PTA is not giving as much service to children and youth as it should." This technology initiative was deemed the highest and best use of our available funds on our school survey and from feedback from teachers, parents and administrators.

Will we have enough money left over in our budget to cover our other costs after this expenditure?

Yes.  The available monies for the technology project are separate from the funds allocated to cover our operating costs for this years’ budget.  Additionally, we have 50% of our annual operating budget in reserve.  

How was it decided that the PTA funds would be spent on interactive white boards and LCD projectors?

A thorough survey and evaluation was completed in choosing the 2008-2009 class gift.   Please see the class gift update at http://www.mckenziepta.com/Committee_Pages/school_gift.html for more details.   In the survey, it was clear that technology was at the top of the list. Additionally, we heard from teachers, administrators and parents about the importance of technology for 21st century students.  At home and throughout their lives, our children are accustomed to immediate access to electronic inquiry and information.   Twenty first century classrooms need to be equipped to teach children how to exist and thrive in the “wired” world.

Who approved spending the PTA’s funds on interactive white boards and LCD projectors?

The PTA membership at the March 10th meeting voted to approve the funding of the interactive white boards and LCD projectors as it was clearly recognized as an innovative, state-of-the-art tool to enhance 21st century learning.

Will McKenzie support and train teachers?

Yes.  In fact, part of the purchase price includes teacher training as well as the ability to call upon the manufacturer for help as teachers integrate technology into their classrooms.   One of the advantages to deciding to purchase the technology now is that Dr. Welter and District 39 can plan to support the teachers with training and information over the summer and during teacher in-service days throughout the next year.

When will they be installed?

They will be installed over the next five months, as the McKenzie School schedule permits.  They will be ready for use at the beginning of the 2009-2010 school year.
