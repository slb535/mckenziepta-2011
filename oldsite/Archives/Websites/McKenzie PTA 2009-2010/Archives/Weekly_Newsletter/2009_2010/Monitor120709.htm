From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

            We welcome December and all it brings.  With the pretty dusting of snow we got this morning comes added layers of winter wear.  Coats, mitten, scarves, hats, boots, and more!  The layering and "un-layering" of all this gear requires extra time and attention, so it's more important than ever for on-time arrivals each day.  We also ask that all belongings are labeled to avoid the overflowing bin of lost and never-found we seem to accumulate.  Our Daisy Girl Scout troop is going to help organize our lost items; they will be putting theses things on display in the school lobby during the final days before winter break.

            As you know, the Cook County Department of Public Health will be sponsoring a free H1N1 vaccination clinic in each of our schools on Thursday, Dec. 17th. Consent forms and letters of explanation have been emailed to you.  If you are having difficulty downloading your copies, please call or visit our school office.  Whether or not your child is going to participate in the vaccination, it is VERY important that we receive your form no later than Wed., Dec. 9th.  At McKenzie, we will operate the clinic in our small gym. In the next week we have more details to share with you regarding the classroom schedule, should you want to be here to support your child. In the meantime, please feel free to visit the vaccination link at: http://www.wilmette39.org/hottopics/H1N1Vaccine.html.

            The Habit of Mind for this month is Listening with Understanding and Empathy. We know that successful people practice the art of active listening.  They put their energy into understanding the points of view of others.  Indicators of listening behavior are: being able to paraphrase another person's ideas, empathizing with their feelings, and accurately expressing another person's concepts.  We find that some children have a difficult time listening to the ideas of others.  They cannot build upon or consider the merits of another person's ideas.  You will know your child is becoming a better listener when he/she can attend to another person, build upon an idea, or give an example of it. You can help at home by asking your child questions such as, "What did your friend think/feel when....?"  "If you were ___, how do you think he/she would feel?" Check out the new Habits of Mind posters that are on display in our main hallways. They are a wonderful reminder to us all!

With best regards,

Dr. Welter
