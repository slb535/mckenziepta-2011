Spanish Newsletter!

March

Spanish is all around McKenzie!  There isn’t a day that goes by without a new word being learned or a new song being sung!

Kindergarten: The Kindergarteners are well into their unit on numbers.  Everyday we practice counting up higher and higher!  The students have had the chance to listen to books about counting and play games that help them identify the Spanish numbers.  The students love singing their number song!  We continue to practice the date, which also gives the students another opportunity to say their numbers.  Practice with your child at home by having them count out objects in Spanish.

1st Grade:  The first graders just finished their numbers unit and are now using their knowledge to study dinero (money).  They began the unit by comparing different types of currency such as dollars and pesos. In class, the students are beginning to practice phrases such as ¿Cuánto cuesta? (How much does it cost?) and Cuesta ___ pesos. (it costs ___ pesos).  They are having so much fun acting as customers and store managers to practice their Spanish knowledge.  Ask your child to count as high as they can in Spanish!

2nd Grade: The second grade students finished their weather unit with great success!  They ended their unit by becoming weather forecasters.  We turned the second grade classrooms into news stations, and your children forecasted the weather for a city of their choice… with a real microphone and everything!  They answered questions such as “¿Qué tiempo hace?” (What’s the weather?) and “¿Hace buen tiempo o hace mal tiempo?” (Is it good or bad weather?).  They were such naturals that Srta. Whitman told them she might see them on television one day in the future!  After their weather unit, the students jumped right into a mini-unit on ropa (clothing).  They had so much fun playing games to learn new vocabulary such as camisa (shirt), traje de baño (bathing suit), and zapatos (shoes).  Challenge your child by asking him/her to tell you about his/her own clothing en español. 

3rd Grade:  The third grade students have sure been busy.  After they presented bilingual living museum projects, they went right into their unit on geometry.  They began the unit by reviewing colors and learning geometrical shapes.  The students played games to answer questions such as ¿Cuántos lados tiene? (How many sides does it have?) and ¿Qué formas hay? (What shapes are there?) It’s incredible to listen to them answering these questions accurately and in complete sentences!  Recently, the students also used digital voice recorders for a listening activity.  Ask your child to tell you all about their technologically advanced activity!

4th Grade: Fourth graders are getting so good at Spanish that they are working on fine-tuning their speaking and written skills! We had a mini-unit on grammar to help us clarify the many aspects of the language we have been using since Kindergarten.  Now, they are enthusiastically busy describing houses and what can be found in them.  We’ve learned how to answer questions like:  ¿Cuántos (insert room here) hay en tu casa? And ¿Qué hay en la casa?  They are getting so good at speaking in complete sentences that Sra. Kus challenged them to “Scattergories”, a sentence writing game describing a picture of a bedroom.  The number of grammatically correct sentences they came up with was astounding!  Ask your students to describe their bedroom at home to you!

Lastly, your students may have told you some news about Sra. Kus.  For those of you who are wondering – yes, it’s true!  She and her husband are thrilled to share that they are expecting a baby this summer!
