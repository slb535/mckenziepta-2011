From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

It was great to see so many families at this week’s Open House!  I think we may have set a new record for the number of people in attendance.  Judging from your very positive comments, the evening was huge success!  A BIG thank you to the McKenzie staff for all their hard work in preparing for this wonderful event, and to you for taking time to celebrate your child’s learning.

It is hard to believe that the month of March is just a week away. From March 1st through the 12th, students in Grades 3-8 across the state of Illinois will be participating in the Illinois State Assessment Tests (ISAT).  We administer one test per day so that the majority of the day remains a typical school day.  Because we test in the morning, we ask that you avoid tardy arrivals, doctor appointments, and/or vacations. We thank you, in advance, for your cooperation.  As always, results are shared with parents in the fall of the next school year.  I am very happy to report that McKenzie School just received the Illinois State Board of Education’s Academic Excellence Award for 2009. To earn this award, 90% of the students must have met or exceeded state standards in both reading and math for at least three consecutive years.  While this is just one measure of success, we are very proud of the hard work it took to earn these fine results!

The District 39 Go Green Committee was formed to address “green” issues and concerns in our school community.  One of the areas identified are water bottles sold in our cafeterias.  They are asking families to please take a moment to share your thoughts about this topic by completing the survey at:  http://www.surveymonkey.com/s/V7R96WQ . Your responses to this survey will assist the Green Committee in making recommendations about the future sale of water bottles in our district.  Thank you very much for your participation. 

Enjoy the weekend!

Spanish Newsletter!

Spanish continues to be spoken throughout McKenzie. Make sure to ask your students all about it! 

Kindergarten: The Kindergarteners are working hard on their clothing unit.  We learned new vocabulary such as “camisa” (shirt), “pantalones” (pants) and “bufanda” (scarf).  The students practiced their Spanish by playing games and listening to Froggy books.  It was fun to incorporate weather and clothing at the same time.  The students will culminate the unit by making a fun and adorable project.  Watch for them in the hallways in the weeks to come!

1st Grade:  The first graders just finished up their community unit.  They have learned several vocabulary words in reference to people and places around their hometown.  In order to practice their conversational skills, the students learned to answer questions such as “¿Qué eres tú?” (What are you? A police officer? A teacher?  A student?) and “¿Dónde estás?” (Where are you?  At school?  At the beach?  At a movie theatre?).  These are great conversational questions to ask your children at home!  We have now moved on to our numbers unit.  The students are reviewing their skills and playing games to eventually learn their numbers up to 100!

2nd Grade:  The second grade students finished their Arctic unit with a bang! The students had the opportunity to take a “trip” to the Arctic.  On their trip, they filled out a travel brochure about the animals they encountered.  Be sure to ask your child to describe his/her favorite animal in Spanish!  The students also created mystery animal projects.  Each student chose their favorite Arctic animal and wrote several complete sentences describing it in Spanish.  Make sure to look for them outside your child’s classroom.  Now, we have begun our weather unit.  We started by reviewing the terminology we use in our daily calendar routine such as “Hace sol” (It’s sunny) and “Hace viento.” (It’s windy).  Over the next few weeks the students will continue practicing their numbers and learn new weather phrases!

3rd Grade:  The third grade students just finished their unit on Explorers!  The students were all asked to go on an expedition by the Queen of Spain.  They were put into groups and discovered a continent from around the world.  Each group wrote about the weather of their continent along with the treasures they found there.  The unit came to an end by a surprise visit from the Queen!  She came to all third grade classrooms, and the students were required to present her with the information from their travels.  It was such a fun way to end our unit!  Now we are into our biography unit.  The students are practicing asking and answering questions about themselves such as “¿Cuándo naciste?” (When were you born?) and “¿Dónde vives?” (Where do you live?) The third graders also had the opportunity to incorporate Spanish into their living museum as they pretended to be their historical individual.  It’s incredible how much they have learned over the past few weeks!

4th Grade:  Fourth graders are about to wrap up their study of the regions of the world.  First, students used their science classification skills to learn and review vocabulary of weather, climate, animals and plants.  They then were able to rely on their large vocabulary base to work on reading and conversation skills.  In groups, students read “magazine articles” about a particular region about which they were to become experts.  A large part of our conversation will occur as we discuss the collages we are creating about our specific regions.  Our unit will culminate in a gallery walk in which students will share facts about their regions with members from other groups.  The collages are looking fantásticos!
