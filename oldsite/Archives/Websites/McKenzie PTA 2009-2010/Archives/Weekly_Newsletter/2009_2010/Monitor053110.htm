Spanish Newsletter

It’s hard to believe the school year is coming to an end!  This will be our last Spanish newsletter of the school year.  Make sure to encourage your children to sing their Spanish songs and practice over the summer!  Have a wonderful break and we’ll see you next year!

Kindergarten: The Kindergarteners have been learning about the country of Guatemala.  A few weeks ago, the students read the story Sawdust Carpets.  This introduced the Guatemalan custom of using sawdust, flowers and pine needles to create “carpets” on the streets before processionals.  The students learned how the Alfombras are made in real life using sawdust and sifters.  Now, the students are ending the year by making their own Alfombras.  Each student chose their own shape to trace and is decorating his/her Alfombra with colored squares.  Look out for those to be coming home at the end of the year!

1st Grade: The first graders have been busy working on their time and daily activities unit.  Each first grade class is practicing telling time in Spanish and learning to say daily activities such as Me levanto. “I wake up,” Voy a la escuela. “I go to school.”  and Me acuesto. “I go to sleep.”  The first graders are making daily routine posters that help them illustrate and explain their day.  We will end the year by having the students practice telling each other about their own daily routines in Spanish!  Make sure to have your children show-off what they have learned at home.  

2nd Grade: The second graders have been learning about butterflies by reviewing their body parts and life cycle.  In order to end the school year in a big way, the students are going to be putting on the play “La Oruga Hambrienta”  “The Hungry Caterpillar” in Spanish.  The students are taking several days to practice the pronunciation and become experts at their own lines.  The play is a wonderful way to show the students knowledge of the lifecycle of a butterfly.  The students also took the time to go over “play etiquette” and what it takes to be an effective actor/actress (speaking loudly, stage presence etc.) The play will be shown to another class in the school and will be a fun way to show off how much the students have learned.  Srta. Whitman is so impressed with the year they have had and know they will all be successful in third grade Spanish!

3rd Grade:  All third grade classes just finished up their unit on Chicago.  The students took the time to learn how to say famous landmarks in Spanish such as El Acuario “ The Aquarium,” El Edificio John Hancock  “ The John Hancock Building,” and La Torre del Agua “The Water Tower.”  The students worked hard to speak and write in complete sentences in order to describe this wonderful city so close to home.  The third graders are ending the year with a mini unit on Fairy Tales.  This will give the students a fun and educational way to read/discuss Fairy Tales in English and Spanish.  Over the summer, please encourage your students to visit our websites and speak with each other en español.

4th Grade:  That….Was….Jeopardy!!!  Fourth graders exceeded Sra. Kus’ expectations during the Planet Jeopardy game!  Everyone really knew their planet facts AND how to discuss them in complete sentences.  Also impressive was the teamwork and good sportsmanship.  We took a break from planets and learned body part vocabulary so that we could create our own aliens.  While we played Simón dice (Simon says), Siéntate and other fun games, we became really good at identifying and saying various body parts.  Students were able to write a description of their alien and detail which planet the alien was from as a final project.  It’s incredible how far these fourth graders have come this year!  We will certainly miss them, but we are confident that they have a strong foundation of language learning skills - whether they continue with Spanish next year or not.  Buena Suerte en Highcrest, 4th graders!
