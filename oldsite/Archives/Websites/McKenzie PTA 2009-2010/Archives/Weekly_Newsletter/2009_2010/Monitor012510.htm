Spanish Newsletter!

So far we’ve had a great 2010 at McKenzie!  Your students are constantly speaking in Spanish and learning new words everyday.

Kindergarten: The Kindergarten students are well into their weather unit.  We have reviewed vocabulary such as  “Hace frío” (It’s cold) and “Hace calor” (It’s hot).  We are singing songs and playing games in order to help us learn new vocabulary.  The students are excelling in this unit because they are already familiar with the terminology thanks to our calendar routine at the beginning of every lesson.  The students will then move on to learn about types of clothing!  Be sure to ask your student “¿Qué tiempo hace hoy?”  (What is the weather like today?). 

1st Grade:  The first graders finished off their tamales unit before break.  They learned the steps to make tamales and were sent home with a recipe that you can make or discuss with your children at home.  We have now moved into our Mexico unit.  We have begun to practice our map skills by learning the cardinal directions in Spanish “Norte” (North),  “Sur”  (South),  “Este”  (East), and “Oeste” (West).  The students are discussing the difference between real life directions and map directions.  We looked at the map of Mexico and discussed the difference between a “ciudad” (city) and a “capital” (capital).  For the remainder of the month, the students will be working on their community unit (learning vocabulary for people and places around their home town). 

2nd Grade:  Before break, the second graders finished their unit on the Incas!  We even had an assembly where the students listened to Andean music and could see the Incan instruments they were learning about in class.  Now we have moved on to our Arctic unit.  The second grade students learned how to say several Arctic animals in Spanish such as “oso polar” (polar bear) and “zorro ártico” (arctic fox).  Now they are working hard on their conversational skills by asking and answering questions to describe the animals.  Some of our favorites so far are “¿Qué color es? (What color is it?) and “¿Tiene cola? (Does it have a tail?) It’s amazing how quickly they are learning to speak in complete sentences…all in Spanish! 

3rd Grade:  The third grade students are becoming true Explorers as we learn about world geography!  All classes have practiced naming the seven continents in Spanish along with the oceans.  Students have also reviewed how to say the cardinal directions and use them on a map.  In preparation to “explore” the world, the students have also learned how to say various treasures such as “oro” (gold)  “plata” (silver) and “cobre” (copper).  Throughout the remainder of the month, the students will talk to their classmates about their travels and continue to practice speaking in complete sentences.  Ask your child to tell you all about their travels!

4th Grade: Fourth graders took a trip to the Galapagos Islands off the coast of Ecuador to do some research on the various animals there.  The students were excellent researchers and gathered a ton of fabulous info, which they then used to write an article for National Geographic.  Students should be very proud of their hard work!  These research packets and articles along with the corresponding rubrics went home the first week back to school.  Also during that first week, students watched DVD’s of the various world language classes offered to them next year at Highcrest. Now we are beginning a unit on the regions of the world.  Weather and climate is being reviewed so that we can identify which types of weather are found in specific regions.  Students can also perform a rap that helped them learn the South American countries and capitals.
