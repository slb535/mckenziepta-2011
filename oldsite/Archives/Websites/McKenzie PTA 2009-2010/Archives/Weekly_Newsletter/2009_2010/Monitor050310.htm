From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

May has arrived with a flurry of activities.  The first week of the month is dedicated to staff appreciation.  It was in 1953 that Eleanor Roosevelt persuaded Congress to declare a National Teachers’ Day.  Since then, schools have designated the first week in May as a time to celebrate and thank the many people who make a difference in our schools. Thanks to the generosity of our PTA, McKenzie staff will be showered with thoughtfulness throughout the week, culminating in a wonderful luncheon on Friday (see PTA News). On behalf of the entire McKenzie Staff, I want to offer our sincere thanks—not only for this special week, but for your ongoing support all year long!

Our online student newspaper is preparing to post its final theme for this year.  Cheetah Chatter hopes the recent EARTH WEEK events will inspire our students to submit articles on how to help our planet GO GREEN. They may write poems, create ads, tell stories, and more, throughout the month of May.

Fourth grade parents are reminded that today was the deadline for submitting paperwork and payment for this year’s Outdoor Education.  Also, second grade dental forms must be submitted to our Health Office no later than May 15th.

Third grade will be celebrating Heritage Day on Thursday afternoon, May 6th.  I look forward to seeing many of you at this wonderful event.  In the meantime, enjoy the weekend!

Best regards,

Dr. Welter
