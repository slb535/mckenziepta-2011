From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

We want to thank the Wilmette Fire Department for helping us kick off Fire Safety Month.  Their informative presentations helped remind us of ways in which we can remain safe at home and at school.  Thanks, also, to all the parents who attended the PLN event featuring one of the authors of the bestseller, Nurture Shock. If you have been unable to participate in the previous PLN events, there are still several learning opportunities in store.  Please note that the video discussion planned for Oct. 7th has been rescheduled for Oct. 21st.  We look forward to seeing you then! 

Environmental Awareness Week is here!  Our PTA has planned numerous activities to raise our awareness (see below).  We will celebrate the week with a Ricky Recycling Assembly on Friday.

Be sure to mark your calendar for Oct. 16th when McKenzie will hold one of its favorite events, Fall Frenzy. We will have everything from pumpkin decorating, to crafts and games. Many hands ensure this evening’s success.  Additional volunteers are still needed to work shifts and bake cakes.  Please stop by the bulletin board in the vestibule of the main entrance, where you will find a master sign-up sheet.

We have begun exploring our first Habit of Mind, Persisting.  Students who are able to persist do so because they have developed a wide range of problem-solving strategies.  Instead of quickly giving up, saying, “I can’t do this!” they find other ways to accomplish their tasks.  At school and at home, we want to build this habit, encouraging our students to keep trying.  If a problem arises, ask your child to describe multiple ways to solve it. 

Wishing you a wonderful week—persist on!

Dr. Welter

Principal

From the P.T.A.

This is Environmental Awareness week.  Activities include: Lobby Collections for your Recyclables all week, a McKenzie Kids Care Poll on Monday, From Trash to Treasure: A Recycled Art Gallery in the Lobby on Tuesday, International Walk to School Day on Wednesday, the Halloween Costume Exchange on Thursday, and Recycling Friday.  For more information please click here.

McKenzie Grocery Dollars:  The McKenzie PTA is the beneficiary of Dominick’s Back to Schools Program.  From 9/18-10/13 simply visit www.backtoschools.escrip.com and enter the redemption code on the bottom of your Dominick’s receipt.  Our group number is 500013854.  Or….send the redemption code to the Grocery Dollars box and we’ll enter it for you!

Avoid the lines!  Pre-order your tickets and pizza for Fall Frenzy, Friday, October 16th from 5:30 pm-8:00 pm.  Order forms are on the website - or contact FF Chair Karen Dekker (ckdekker@comcast.net) for more information.  Don’t miss out on this special McKenzie event!

Save the Date for Ladypalooza 2009, November 6th at the Wilmette Golf Club.  Please join us for an evening of shopping, exciting raffle packages and informal modeling, while socializing all in support of McKenzie.  Beverages and appetizers will be served throughout the evening.  For more information or to help with the event, please contact Melissa Hirschel at mirhirschel@msn.com or Hilary Ashlund-Mazzier at finitable@sbcglobal.net.

Show Your Spirit!  Please check out McKenzie Spirit wear located on the McKenzie PTA website.  It is an online site that you can order from at any time.  The offerings include apparel, hats, totes, buttons and notecards.  Any questions, please contact Jane O’Malley: janeomalley@comcast.net.

As part of their series of events for parents and their teens regarding Internet use, the Wilmette Junior High School PTO and the Family Awareness Network have brought Dr. danah boyd, co-author of Hanging Out, Messing Around, Geeking Out: Kids Living and Learning with New Media, and nationally recognized expert in the area of social networking to speak at the Wilmette Jr. High School auditorium on October 7th at 7:00 pm.

Fall Frenzy needs YOU!  Shifts still need to be filled.  Please bake a cake or work a shift at this fun, family-oriented event.  Serve pizza, work a craft, run a game, etc.- there’s something for everyone!  Sign-up sheets in front of the vestibule-or contact Sonja Iribarten at sonjabeals@gmail.com to help!

McKenzie Kids Growing in the Gardens: Did you know that the gardens are open for students to explore during recess on Fridays during September and October?  Students can spend all or part of their recess exploring in the prairie and butterfly gardens, drawing, painting , helping with the compost, watering the plants, raking, or filling out garden reports  If you would like to volunteer to help in the gardens during recess, please email Kirsten Stadheim at stadheimwefing@sbcglobal.net.
