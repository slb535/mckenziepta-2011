From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

Happy New Year to all!  I hope your holidays were enjoyable and that you are ready for a fun and busy 2010.  This month we feature a new Habit of Mind, Thinking Flexibly. Research tells us that successful people know how to consider alternative points of view.  They are interested in knowing that there is more than one solution to a problem.  As children become more flexible in their thinking they learn to appreciate divergent thinking.  We teach children to understand and use expressions such as, “On the other hand” and “If you look at it another way….”  In the coming weeks, be sure to discuss with your child how thinking flexibly helps you handle situations in your career and/or home. 

We are currently collecting student writing for the second edition of our student newspaper, Cheetah Chatter (see below).  This online publication features the writing of students from all grade levels.  I hope you will encourage you child to submit an entry soon!

January and February are busy months for our PTA.  The next PTA meeting will be held on Tuesday at 9:15, with a feature presentation by our foreign language teachers.  Plans are underway for the 30th Annual Variety Show, bringing an outstanding anniversary edition of this signature event, March 15-20th.  You should have already received your electronic invite to this year’s Bloomin’ Benefit.  For the past several months, this PTA Committee has been hard at work, preparing a wonderfully good time for us on Feb. 6th.  I do hope you will be able to join in the fun!  And just when you thought there wasn’t anything left to do, our yearly Book Fair comes to town on Feb. 9-11th.  Be sure to scroll down to read more about these and all the other PTA happenings.

I close with a reminder to all parents about the (joys) challenges that snowy weather brings.  We must insist that parents do not leave their cars unattended in the drop-off lane in front of school.  This interrupts the traffic flow and creates potential accidents.  Also, we need your help in supervising students who choose to play on school property after school hours.  Snowball throwing in not permitted—before, during, or after school.  We have had reports of students being injured by rough play that can often be curtailed by parents’ watchful eyes.  Thank you, in advance, for your assistance with this, as it appears that the snow will definitely be here for quite awhile!

Wishing you a terrific weekend!

Dr. Welter
