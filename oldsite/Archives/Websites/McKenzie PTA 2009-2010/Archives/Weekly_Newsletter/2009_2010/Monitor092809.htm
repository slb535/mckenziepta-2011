From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

On Thursday our students celebrated Olympics Day in Wilmette by participating in an assembly that featured former Olympian, Doc Nicholson. He shared some of his experiences as a heavyweight boxer and explained the virtues that are represented by the six points of the Olympic star---hope, excellence, respect, harmony, friendship, and celebration!  We now await the Oct. 2nd decision to find out just how “close to home” the 2016 events will be!

Thank you to the parents who helped us with school pictures last week.  If your child was absent, or you are not pleased with the photos you will receive, re-takes will be available on Oct. 27th.

Thank you to everyone who contributed to the Back-to-School Supply drive!  Students are putting all of those donations to good use at Carman-Buckner, our sister school.

As we continue to settle into this new year, I would like to call your attention to our school rule regarding the use of bikes, scooters, etc.  Once students reach school property, they are asked to dismount their equipment and walk the rest of the way.  This helps ensure the safety of everyone on our busy sidewalks and playground.

Wishing you a wonderful week of learning!

Dr. Welter

Principal
