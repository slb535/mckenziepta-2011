From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

Halloween is fast approaching, and with it comes questions about our school “happenings”.  Because our celebration is occurring on an early release day, we will have only a morning parade, beginning at 9:15 (weather-permitting).  Students are allowed to wear their costumes to school this day.  As always, we ask that costumes are age appropriate, with no blood, gore, pretend weapons, or otherwise frightening accessories. Please save the hair dyes and make-up for trick or treating, as they become huge distracters in the classroom.   Parents and other guests are invited to watch the parade from the sidewalk and/or blacktop, as we go out the front entrance, along the north side of the school, then across the blacktop and around to the front of the building.  Afternoon kindergartners may participate in the parade if accompanied by a parent.  With 22 classrooms on display this will be one very long parade! We are asking our viewing audience to understand that classes will be unable to stop along the way for individual or class photos.  Some classes may choose to assemble for a class photo after they have completed the parade.  We encourage you to take advantage of before and after school for photo opportunities on the school grounds.  Dismissal on Oct. 30th will take place at 12:15 pm for Gr. 1-4; 10:20 am for AM Kindergarten.

 Picture Re-Takes are scheduled for Tuesday, the 27th.  Please remember to return your picture package and/or the payment envelope if your child is planning to be photographed.  We continue to persist in practicing safety on school grounds.  Please remind your child to dismount his/her wheeled vehicle once on school property.  This will help prevent accidents.

 I have the privilege of attending the 21st Century Teaching and Learning Conference in Washington DC on Thursday through Sunday of this coming week.  At the conference we will explore education practices that enable us to provide a 21st century education for all students. I look forward to bringing back new ideas to share with you in the months to come.  Mrs. Anne Bowers and other district administrators will be available to assist you in my absence.

Have a SPOOKTACULAR Halloween, all!

 Dr. Welter
