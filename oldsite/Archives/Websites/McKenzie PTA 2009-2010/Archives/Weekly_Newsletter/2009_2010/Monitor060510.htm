From the Principal

 

Dear McKenzie Families,

 

The end has arrived—and how quickly it did!  It’s hard to believe this school year has come to a close.  We have so much to remember and to celebrate.  Thank you to the entire McKenzie Staff and to our supportive parents who complete our TEAM.  A well-deserved summer break will renew our energy and enthusiasm for a new year filled with ideas and events! 

 

Next week~Join in the fun for SPIRIT WEEK:

Monday: Wear your favorite color Day

Tuesday:  College Day

Wednesday: McKenzie Blue & White Day

 

Before we go…

 

*If you have not yet gone online to register for next school year, we strongly encourage you take a moment to do so today.  We still have 200 families who have not yet registered, leaving us wondering just how many students to plan for in the coming year.  The early-bird discount expires on June 9th.  Don’t delay!

 

*We still have 95 books that have not yet been returned to our school library. Please be sure to return all books on Monday so we can complete our inventory.  

 

*Be sure to check out the LOST & Never-FOUND table set up in our lobby.  Any items unclaimed by Wednesday will be donated or discarded.

 

*Remove ALL items from your child’s locker.  If you think stray socks can “wreak” havoc in lockers, you haven’t experienced half-eaten sandwiches and snacks!  Please help us prepare our building for summer school and eventually our usual "power-cleaning".

 

At this time we bid a fond farewell to the following McKenzie staff:

Ms. Elissa Cornyn, Kindergarten

Ms. Katie Heidecker, (Mrs. Freed/ Wade’s substitute)

Ms. Peggy Power, LBS

Mrs. Cindy Dougherty, LBS

Ms. Jenny Orfuss, Speech-Language

Mrs. Jodi Cloutier, DST

Mr. Martin Keane, paraprofessional

We thank them for their service and wish them all the best!

 

We also say a bittersweet farewell to our fourth graders and their families. This amazing group of children who started small, but grew mighty, are ready for all that Highcrest and life has to offer.  Their collective wisdom and generous spirit will be missed.

 

In closing…..

While the District 39 summer school program and park district programs will be housed at McKenzie this summer, our secretaries will be taking some time off.  Our office will close on June 16th, and will resume operation on Monday, August 2nd. For immediate assistance between June 17-Aug. 1st, please call the M.E.C. @ 847-256-2450.

 

As always, it has been my pleasure learning with you this year!  Together, we have shaped lives and left the property at 649 Prairie Avenue a better place.  For that I am most grateful!  Wishing you fun in the sun with family and friends.  May your toes find some sand and your eyes a good book (or two)!  See you at Meet and Greet on August 25th.

 

Best regards,

Dr. Welter

welterd@wilmette39.org

From the P.T.A.

 

Collection for Oil Spill: Student Council is sponsoring a hosiery collection for the oil spill in the gulf.  New or used hosiery should be brought to the school lobby.  The hosiery is cut to make “packets of human hair” which then are used to absorb oil.  This collection will continue until the end of the school year.

 

Grocery Dollars Program needs volunteers for deliveries in June.  Please contact Jennifer Lind at ryan6lind@comcast.net if you can assist.

 

Ice Cream Social Committee 2010: Delicious ice cream, family, friends, crafts, and an afternoon of all sorts of entertaining fun.  Does that sound like a great time to you? While we are just on the brink of summer vacation, it is never too late to start thinking about our back-to-school Ice Cream Social. If you are interested in helping to plan a fantastic afternoon of fun for the whole family, please contact Rachel Arend at rachel.arend@gmail.com.

 

Join the Yearbook Team! Join us as we put together the 2010-2011 Yearbook.  Would you like to take charge of the Cover Contest, which is held in the late autumn?  Or perhaps you want to help with the famous Fourth Grade Center Spread?  Contact Sara Kurensky (sara@kurensky.org) and let her know what you want to do!

 

2010-2011 Room Parent Sign Up: We are already looking toward next year for Room Parent sign up and we are taking it electronic!  No Backpack Express needed!  Please click here to fill in the few fields and hit submit!  It is that easy.  If you have trouble, please feel free to email Anne Edmondson at  a.creative@comcast.net with any questions you might have.  

 

Thank you to McKenzie Garden Volunteers!  In addition to parent volunteers during recess and the 4th grade class gift planting project, numerous people have given their time pulling weeds and donating plants from their own gardens, and students have labored in the gardens on Fridays.  We look forward to expanding recess days in the fall.  Thanks also to Millen Hardware and West End Florist & Nursery for significant discounts on items purchased for our garden expansion project.

From District 39…

 

The D39 Educational Foundation Board of Trustees met this week to wrap up the 2009-2010 school year and start planning for 2010-2011.  Here are some updates:

 

Save the date:  Saturday, September 25 is circus day in Wilmette, with two performances scheduled on the field behind the Mikaelian Education Center. Ticket order forms will be available at www.d39foundation.org later this summer.

 

Thank you for your support of our 2010 Distinguished Citizen, Walter Reed! We had a wonderful turnout on May 18th for this remarkable man, with over 180 D39 families and friends on hand to celebrate Walter's award.

 

D39 families have great things to say about our teachers!  Please visit our online Wall of Honor to see how we appreciate amazing D39 teachers http://www.wilmette39.org/d39foundation/teacher-appreciation-wall.html

Leslie Weyhrich (chair)

Foundation liaisons for 2010-11 will be:  Mary Foster (WJHS), ME Girgenti (HMS), Kathleen Boehm (Central), Kim Ratkin (McKenzie), Brenda Blomberg (Romona), Anne West (Harper).

 

Congratulations to all the teachers and students involved in the Foundation's Gripp Grant program this year.  We awarded 10 grants in 2009-2010, and look forward to the next round of innovative grants in the fall.  Gripp Grant deadlines will be October 15, 2010 and February 15, 2011.
