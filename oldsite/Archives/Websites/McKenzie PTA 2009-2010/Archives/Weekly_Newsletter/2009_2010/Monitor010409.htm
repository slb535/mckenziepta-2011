From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

It was a busy week, indeed!  All is definitely merry and bright!  There has been gingerbread decorating, holiday sing-a-longs, and parties galore.  We also managed to "squeeze in" a very successful H1N1 clinic.  As Dr. Lechner's email stated, thank you to the parents and all the staff who helped out.  The day ran very smoothly as we vaccinated 280 children and adults.

As we look forward to all the wonderful celebrations over the next two weeks, I want to wish you the best both during the holidays and in the New Year.  Before you know it, January 4, 2010 will be here!  In the meantime, enjoy the gift of time with family and friends~

Sincerely,

Dr. Welter

IN OTHER NEWS.....

Calling all Snow Sculptors!

Did you know …

When you build a snowman or create a snow fort you are being a sculptor?

You are invited to take a digital picture of your snowman and send it to my email address: porterc@wilmette39.org I will be posting your creations on my school website.  Also, images will be on display in the hall in a Virtual Snow Sculpture Gallery.

 

We have Exciting News on the yearbook front - it is now possible for everyone in the McKenzie community to upload photos directly to the secure yearbook website.  Which means each of you can help make this year's McKenzie Memories the best ever! 

It's easy - just go to the Yearbook Committee page on the PTA website and follow the simple directions:  http://www.mckenziepta.com/_yearbook.html

There are only a few rules: School related pictures only please - no scouts, sports teams, or non-PTA events.  And no cell-phone pictures.  Most cameras will work fine - if you're not sure, upload your picture and we'll take it from there.   

We wanted to let you know about this now, just in case any of you are looking for something to do over winter break (!) - plus it gives us a chance to wish each of you a Happy New Year.  

Best, 

The Yearbook Committee

(Questions? sara@kurensky.org, 847-251-1925)

From the P.T.A.

Please join us at the Tuesday, January 12th PTA meeting at 9:15 am.  Our highlighted topic is the District 39 Foreign Language Program.  We’ll enjoy two sets of speakers, our very own McKenzie Spanish Teachers as well as the Foreign Language Content Area Assistant (5th-8th) and a 5th/6th Grade Spanish Teacher.  Whether your child is in Kindergarten or headed off to Highcrest soon, come learn more about the Foreign Language Program in District 39.

Save the date!  The Parent Learning Network (PLN) invites you to attend a thought-provoking discussion on Tuesday, January 26th from 9:30-11:00 am in the McKenzie School library.  An article featuring an interview with Sir Ken Robinson, and international expert on innovation, will be the focus of our discussion.  Stay tuned for more information.

In the Community:  On January 18, 2010 in honor of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., The Interfaith Housing Center of the Northern Suburbs and The Volunteer Center of New Trier Township are sponsoring a daylong event featuring various service projects including making fleece no-sew blankets for charities such as Project Linus.  The daylong event will also include entertainment throughout the day.  For more information, email VCHelps@aol.com or phone Interfaith Housing Center, 847-501-5760.

Save the Date: Open Gym Night at McKenzie- January 13th from 6:00-8:00 pm.

Reminder: Submissions for Monitor are due by Thursday at 12:00 pm.  Thanks!
