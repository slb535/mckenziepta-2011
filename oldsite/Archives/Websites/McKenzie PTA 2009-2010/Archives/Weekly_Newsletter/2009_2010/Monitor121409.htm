Spanish Newsletter!

  It’s hard to believe that the snow is falling and December is already here!  Your students are learning more and more Spanish everyday!  Have a wonderful holiday season! 

§  Kindergarten: The Kindergarteners have just finished their unit on people and places around the school.  We sang many songs and played lots of games to reinforce vocabulary they can relate to on a daily basis.  We are jumping right into the next unit, which is “The Body.”  The Kindergarteners are learning how to say words such as manos “hands”, boca “mouth”, and pies “feet”.  Make sure to ask your child how to sing “Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes” in Spanish!   

§  1st Grade: The first graders have successfully completed their unit on habitats.  The students are experts at naming different types of animals and the habitats in which they live.  We read Diary of a Worm in Spanish and then made our own books.  Each student’s worm visited numerous habitats and encountered a different animal in each one.  Have your child read you their book in Spanish that came home recently.  The students also finished a mini-unit entitled “Patio de mi casa.”  For a few days, the students learned how to say objects that can be found in their own backyards- another great discussion piece!  We will be finishing the month by learning about Tamales.  Your students will learn how tamales are an important part of the Mexican culture along with the steps needed to make them!  Yum!

§  2nd Grade: Second grade Spanish students performed their presentations of “La Mariquita Malhumorada” (The Grouchy Ladybug- by Eric Carle). Srta. Whitman was so impressed by how many actors and actresses there are at McKenzie!  After the play, we jumped right into our unit on Peru/The Incas.  The students have been pretending to be an Inca and actually received their own Inca names.  The students learned about the Inca lifestyle such as the instruments they played, where they lived, and the counting system they used.  The students actually got to hear someone speaking “Quechua,” the language of the Incas, and tried to guess what the words meant in Spanish.  We have had so much fun being Incas!  Make sure to ask your child about their favorite Inca activity.    

§  3rd Grade:  The third graders have been extremely busy in their plant unit.  The students are experts at the names of vegetables such as cebolla “onion,” pimiento “pepper,” and lechuga “lettuce.”  The third grade students have also become familiar with words associated with cooking such as mezcla “mix,” corta “cut,” and pon “put.”  Last week, the students worked in pairs to create their own vegetable soup recipe.  This past week, they had a few days to practice saying and acting out their recipes before presenting their recipes on the “Food Network de Español!”  Make sure you ask your third grader to act out their soup recipe for you at home!

§  4th Grade: Fourth graders completed their Ocean Animals unit with a press conference in which they asked and answered questions about their animal discovery – all in español!  Around Thanksgiving, we learned a couple new questions to ask about animals as we studied food chains and learned a little about animal adaptations.  Watch out, Charles Darwin!  You may have discovered some animals and their adaptations in the Galapagos Islands, but McKenzie students can talk about all of them en español!  This past week, we traveled to Las Islas Galápagos in order to learn about the fascinating variety of animals found there.  Ask your student to tell you all about the process of research there and the animals he/she learned about.  You will be as amazed by their knowledge as by their Spanish skills.
