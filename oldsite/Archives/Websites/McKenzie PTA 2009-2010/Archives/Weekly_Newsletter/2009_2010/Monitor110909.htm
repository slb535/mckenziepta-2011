From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

Welcome to the busy month of November and the beginning of the second quarter of the year! We look forward to seeing you at Parent Teacher Conferences on Nov. 10-12-13th.  To avoid a conflict with the District 39 Educational Foundation celebration that will be held on Tuesday, Nov. 10th, we have postponed this month’s PTA meeting to Tuesday, Nov. 17th at 7:00 pm.  We will meet in the school library in order to share with you a brief Smart Board demonstration. I hope you will be able to join us.

You may recall that last year, third grader Nicole Basil, along with the assistance of her parents and the wider McKenzie community, organized a used bicycle drive that yielded over 100 bicycles that were donated to inner city children. I am happy to announce that Nicole and her brother, Bennett, are planning a repeat performance and are preparing to host Pedal Power on Nov. 14th.  If you, your friends, or neighbors have spare bicycles that are cluttering your garage, this is a wonderful opportunity to contribute to a great cause! We will be accepting your donations on McKenzie’s blacktop from 9:30-11:30 a.m. next Saturday.  I hope to see many of you there!

This month we are featuring a new Habit of Mind—managing impulsivity.  People who demonstrate this habit think before they act.  They are able to create a plan of action, developing strategies for problem solving. They also look at multiple solutions instead of taking the first suggestion that comes to mind.  We will be reading about and discussing this habit and will share more about this habit with you later this month.

Thank you to all the parents who were able to attend our Parent Education and LadyPalooza events this week.  Our McKenzie “community” is alive and well!

Be sure to scroll down through ALL the news attached to this newsletter.  In addition to school and PTA news, you will find our Spanish teachers’ monthly newsletter.  Happy reading!

Best regards,

Spanish Newsletter

November, 2009

Your children continue to amaze us with their knowledge of Spanish.  It is wonderful to watch them learn and grow as the year goes on.  We hope you had a fun Halloween weekend and have a great start to the holiday season!  =)

Kindergarten: The Kindergarten students have done such a wonderful job of adjusting to Spanish.  We hear them practicing in the hallways and using the language in settings beyond the classroom.  We are currently in our school unit.  The students have learned how to say a variety of school supplies in Spanish along with places in their own school.  In the upcoming weeks we will be learning about the people who work in a school and how to say their names in Spanish.  We have continued to practice the days of the week and numbers whenever we meet.  Be sure to ask your children to teach you some of their favorite school songs!

1st Grade:  The first graders show us how much they are learning on a daily basis.  We have successfully completed our family unit.  The students are experts at describing members of their family- including their pets!  Now we are in the middle of our unit on worms.  The students have already completed an activity where they labeled the parts of a worm.  Make sure to ask your students what a worm needs to stay alive!  This week we are beginning to discuss the different types of worms.  We have played repetition review games in class to help us remember the new vocabulary.  You can ask your student all about Matamoscas (Flyswatter), Envelope Cambio (Envelope Change) and Electricidad (Electricity).

2nd Grade:  The second graders have been extremely busy lately becoming actors and actresses.  The students will soon be performing “La Mariquita Malhumorada,” their first play of the year, for an audience of students from another class.  The play is based on the book, The Grouchy Ladybug by Eric Carle.  The students have been working diligently to learn their lines, practice their parts and discuss what it takes to be an effective actor/actress.  In order to prepare for the play the students learned how to identify the parts of a ladybug in Spanish along with their lifecycle.  Make sure to ask your students about their part in the play!

3rd Grade:  The third grade students have been working hard on their plant unit.  We discussed the things a plant needs in order to live a healthy life (sun, water, soil etc.) and proceeded to learn about the lifecycle of a plant.  The students had the opportunity to create a “Planta Loca” (crazy plant) during class and describe it using their new plant vocabulary.  The students learned the word “hay” (there is/there are) that helped them write in complete Spanish sentences!  This week we are learning how to say various plants/vegetables in Spanish.  This will help them create their own recipe for vegetable soup and present it on their very own cooking show!

4th Grade: Fourth graders have continued to impress Sra. Kus with their amazing Spanish speaking skills!  Many are working hard and succeeding in their quest to speak in complete sentences.  Most of those complete sentences have been all about ocean animals and their characteristics.  Students can ask and answer about a dozen questions about each ocean animal.  At home, you can ask your student to describe his/her favorite animal to you in Spanish.  You won’t believe all the things that they can say!

As always, please feel free to contact us if you have any questions or comments!  If you would like to conference with either one of us, we will be available on Thursday, November 12th and Friday, November 13th.  Please email us to make an appointment.

¡Hasta Pronto!

PEDAL POWER

Are there extra bikes or scooters in your garage that you have outgrown?

Would you like to donate them to kids who don't have a bike or a scooter?

We are collecting bikes and scooters for the Jane Addams Hull House Association in Chicago.  This is a program to recognize achievement and commitment in the Chicago Public Schools.  Many of these kids have never owned a bike.

If you would like to participate, please bring your bike or scooter to the McKenzie Elementary School teacher's parking lot at 649 Prairie Ave. in Wilmette (link for map) on Saturday, November 14 between 9:30 am and 11:30 am.   There is a second drop-off spot for bikes this year in Northbrook at the George Garner Cyclery, if that is more convenient for you (link for map). 

Please make sure the bike or scooter is clean and in good working order.  If you can bring it with a bow already attached, that would be great!  Last year we accepted donated helmets, but this year the Hull House Association is going to buy new helmets for the students for safety and health reasons.  If you would like, you can go to the Hull House Association website and make a donation directly to them at this link.  Thanks!

If you plan to bring a bike or scooter, please call or send an e-mail to our parents.  Or feel free to call us with any questions, or visit our website to learn more about the program, www.gopedalpower.com.
