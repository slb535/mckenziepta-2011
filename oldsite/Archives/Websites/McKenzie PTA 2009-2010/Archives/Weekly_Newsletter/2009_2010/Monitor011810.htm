From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

We continue to work on demonstrating the habit of Thinking Flexibly. In our classrooms, we explored an idea for many angles, trying to make sure our students understood it before forming an opinion about the idea.  Our students are learning that their initial ideas and strategies are not the only way and that being open-minded and creative is a useful skill.  For example, in the early 19th century many people believed the only way humans would ever be able to travel long distances quickly and efficiently was by breeding faster horses.  However, nothing really made long-distance travel faster until the steam train was invented!  One of the world’s leading experts in lateral thinking is Edward de Bono.  His book, Six Thinking Hats, serves as a rich resource of ideas on flexible thinking.  You may want to check it out!

We are looking forward to having The Kindness Connection at McKenzie next week (see below). They will be hosting a variety of service learning projects in each classroom.  In Kindergarten, classes will be decorating placemats for the homebound elderly; First Grade classes will be decorating pencil bags for orphans in Armenia.  Second and Fourth Graders will be making pillows for hospitalized children, while Third Graders will be decorating Spanish bookmarks and place cards for the elderly.  The Kindness Connection is an innovative, not-for-profit organization dedicated to promoting volunteerism by connecting schools, groups, and/or individuals with those in need through service learning projects such as the ones our students will produce.  For more information, be sure to check out their website at www.thekindnessconnection.org.

Don’t forget that the Growing Minds Benefit is fast-approaching.  I encourage you not only to RSVP to the electronic invitation you received, but also be sure to make your payment to confirm your reservation.  This can be done at www.mckenziepta.com.  We look forward to having many parents and staff members join in the fun!

Looking forward to seeing you at many of the upcoming events.  In the meantime, enjoy the long weekend~

Dr. Welter
