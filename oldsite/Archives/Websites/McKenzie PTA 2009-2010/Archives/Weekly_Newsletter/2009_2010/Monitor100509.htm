From the Principal

 

Dear McKenzie Families,

 

As we turn the page on our calendars we welcome cooler temperatures, beautiful fall colors, and all that October has to bring.  On Wednesday of this week we will highlight Fire Safety, with the Wilmette Fire Department conducting presentations for our K-3 students.  We practiced our first fire drill recently and were able to evacuate the building in 70 seconds!  We will conduct other drills In the weeks ahead, rehearsing ways we can be safe.

 

The month of October also features Environmental Awareness Week (Oct. 5-9), Fall Frenzy (Oct. 16), and so much more!  We continue to enjoy outdoor learning with our scientist in residence, Julie Zielke, as well as with field trips to places such as Emily Oaks Nature Center, The Evanston Ecology Center, and the Botanic Gardens.

 

Beginning this week we will be introducing our students to a yearlong study of the Habits of Mind. Considerable research demonstrates that successful people in most any walk of life display a common set of characteristics or dispositions.  They have intelligent patterns of thinking and behaving when confronted with life’s dilemmas and problems. We want our children to develop these “habits” that will lead them to become lifelong learners and effective problem solvers, able to live successfully in a rapidly changing world.

 

To that end, we will feature a different habit each month, using morning announcements, hallway displays, and classroom instruction. As our students acquire a common vocabulary, they will learn new strategies to use now and in the future.  During the month of October we will focus on the habit of Persisting. Over next several weeks I will continue to address this topic, sharing some of our new learning and offering ways in which you might reinforce these concepts at home.  Together we can support these behaviors so that they truly become “habits” for a lifetime.  For more information on the Habits of Mind, visit: www.habits-of-mind.net.

 

Dr. Welter

Principal
