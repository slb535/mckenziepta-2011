From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

I am writing to you from Washington D.C., learning so many new things at the national conference on 21st Century Teaching and Learning!  I hope the weather has improved, allowing for some trick-or-treating fun today.  I’m sorry that Mother Nature didn’t cooperate with our school’s plan for a parade.  Thank you for your understanding and for modeling for our children how we make the best of disappointing situations—the Habits of Mind in “real time”!

The first quarter of the school year has now come to a close.  We look forward to sharing your child’s progress at Parent-Teacher Conferences on Nov. 12-13 (and Nov. 10th for Kindergarten).  There are still times slots available.  Please don’t delay in arranging your appointment, as registration will close on Friday, Nov. 6th, at 10:00 am. 

It’s not too late for McKenzie moms to purchase a ticket for this week’s LadyPalooza. Come join the fun on Friday, Nov. 6th (see below).   

The District 39 Educational Foundation will bestow its second annual Distinguished Citizen Award to Chef Charlie Trotter, a renowned D39 alumnus and a former Wilmette resident.  This event will take place on Tuesday, November 10th at Michigan Shores Club from 6:30pm to 9:00pm, and you are most certainly invited to attend!   In fact, by now you should have received your personal invitation in the mail.  If this is not at your fingertips, you can find all event details on the District 39 Educational Foundation website, http://www.wilmette39.org/d39foundation/distinguished-citizen-award.html.  You can also download the order form directly from this website by visiting  http://www.wilmette39.org/d39foundation/CharlieTrotterNov10-ticket-order.pdf. Hope you can attend.

I know many of you are concerned about the health of your child and H1N1. Please be sure to read Dr. Lechner's email about H1N1. If you can't locate it in your inbox, you can find it on the D39 website.  Even though most doctors are not testing for H1N1, we do know that we have a few diagnosed cases in our school. We are being told that this is “the flu" of the season and that the large majority of flu cases are H1N1.  As you know, we have stepped up the cleaning practices in the building and classrooms.  We are being diligent about hand washing. Unfortunately, with 540 students and nearly 100 staff, viruses/bacteria do enter the building. Students with a fever are to be kept home for 24 hours AFTER they are fever free (with no medication assisting the decreased fever). For more details, please go to the D39 website:  http://wilmette39.org

 

Best regards,

Dr. Welter

From the P.T.A.

LadyPalooza 2009 on November 6th from 6:00 pm – 10:00 pm at the Wilmette Golf Club.  It’s girls night out.  Mix and mingle while raising money for the McKenzie PTA.  Enjoy catering by Open Kitchen and shopping with 23 local businesses.  Take a chance with our raffle and enjoy some informal modeling and mini spa treatments.

Come one, come all!  Join the Parent Learning Network (PLN) in the McKenzie Library on Wednesday, November 4th from 7:00 – 9:00 pm for Part 2 of the videotaped lecture of Alfie Kohn.  He will be speaking on his book Moving from Rewards and Punishments to Reason and Love.  Attending Part 1 is not a prerequisite.  RSVP to Julie Johnson at jujohnson007@sbcglobal.net .  Check the PTA website for more information.

Shopping & Gift Cards: Remember the McKenzie Shopping & Gift Card program for your winter clothes shopping and holiday gift needs.  We have many vendors on hand for immediate purchase.  November orders are due 11/13.  For more information, click here or contact Lia Chong at lia38@yahoo.com or 728-0303.

Date Change: The November PTA Meeting has been switched to Tuesday, November 17th at 7:00 pm in the school library.  The focus of the meeting will be a Smart Board presentation by McKenzie staff.  All are welcome!

Junior Great Books: Registration for Great Books is open from now until November 13th.  You can register thru Backpack Express or on-line at the McKenzie PTA website using PayPal.  This is an 8 week program that students in grades 1-4 can participate in once a week during lunch and recess.  Sessions will begin in January.

Junior Great Books:  We are still in need of parents to teach a second grade group and a fourth grade group.  Please contact Ann Sciortino at 4Sciortino@gmail.com  The program is for 8 weeks beginning in January.

PTA Directories were sent home last week via Backpack Express.  If you have not had the chance to join the PTA and receive your directory, it is not too late!  Go to the McKenzie PTA home page and use the “SHOP AND PAY HERE” button or follow this link to purchase your membership and directory on-line: www.mckenziepta.com/_purchase_page.html1.
