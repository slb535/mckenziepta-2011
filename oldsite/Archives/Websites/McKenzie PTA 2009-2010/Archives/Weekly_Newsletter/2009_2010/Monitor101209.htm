From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

We continue to focus on our Habit of Mind, Persisting. We read about characters who demonstrate this habit and discussed ways in which we can keep trying. Students are encouraged to spend some time this weekend asking an adult to describe a time in which he/she persisted.  I am sure you will have wonderful examples to share with your children.

Thank you to our Environmental Awareness Committee for the activities they provided this week.  From morning announcements to recycling a wide variety of items, our students are learning that we all play an important role in taking care of our planet!

While our students enjoy a school holiday on Monday, the staff will be participating in a host of learning opportunities, focused on meeting the needs of every learner.  Our featured speaker, Dr. Curry, will address the ways in which we can customize education around students’ learning styles, strengths and weaknesses. 

When school resumes on Tuesday we will hold our monthly PTA meeting at 9:15 in the cafeteria.  Our guest speaker is Superintendent Lechner.  McClubs are also now underway.  What a terrific selection of activities we have this session!  If you haven’t yet purchased your tickets for Fall Frenzy, it’s not too late. It’s sure to be a good time for all.  I look forward to seeing you there!

Best regards,

Dr. Welter

McKenzie Kids Growing in the Gardens: Did you know that the gardens are open for students to explore during recess on Fridays during September and October?  Students can spend all or part of their recess exploring in the prairie and butterfly gardens, drawing, painting, helping with the compost, watering the plants, raking, or filling out garden reports.  If you would like to volunteer to help in the gardens during recess, please email Kirsten Stadheim at stadheimwefing@sbcglobal.net.
