From the Principal

Dear McKenzie Families,

As always, we have had a busy week of learning. Some special highlights included a special presentation on the life of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., as part of our third graders’ study of biographies, as well as a visit from children’s author, Debbie Miller, who shared her expert knowledge of Alaska and animal adaptations with our second graders.  These are just two examples of the wonderful enrichment activities that are brought to our students throughout the year through the generosity of our PTA.  I hope your children share the excitement of these events with you!

We are wrapping up our two-week Helping Hands for Haiti fundraiser.  I am happy to report that McKenzie has raised over $3,000 in donations and pretzel sales.  We will continue to sell pretzels in the coming week.  If your child has a donation that has not yet been brought to school, be sure to send it in on Monday.  In addition to all the money that has been raised, McKenzie has certainly showed their SOLE during the shoe drive.  We collected OVER 800 pairs of shoes.  Thank you to all the generous families who contributed new and used shoes, as well as three local shoe stores who overwhelming supported the drive. Complete Birkenstock on Lake Avenue and Waxberg's Walk Shoppe in Niles generously opened their drop boxes for our shoe drive.  Many of the shoes collected will be shipped directly by these stores to the Soles4Souls organization where they will be processed and moved on to Haiti or other parts of the world.  These stores support Soles4Souls year round and you can always make a shoe donation in their stores throughout the year.  Vose Sanders Bootery on Central Street in Evanston joined in the efforts and with the help of Kim Holstein donated 40 pair of beautiful children's shoes.  Please give these stores a McKenzie thank you the next time you visit.

On Monday, report cards will go home in students’ backpacks.  You are no longer being required to sign an envelope showing your receipt of this document, so we want to be sure to remind you to look for the report card envelope. We hope you will find your child's report card to be an informative summary of his/her work during the first semester.  Our teachers work very diligently and thoughtfully to prepare these documents.  Our report cards are part of our reporting system, which includes parent-teacher conferences (fall and spring), and all of the regular communication between the classroom and home. If you need clarification interpreting the report card, please access parent brochures at: http://www.wilmette39.org/curriculum/curriculumhighlights.htm  Scroll down the right side of the page for the brochure that is applicable for your student's grade.

Next week also brings McKenzie’s annual BOOK FAIR as well as an evening PTA Meeting (7:00 pm, cafeteria) where I will be sharing the State of the School address.   I hope many of you will be able to attend. Prior to the PTA Meeting, I will be hosting a brief information session for third grade parents who would like more information regarding CogAt Testing. That will begin at 6:30 pm in the cafeteria.

Thursday, Feb. 11th is the 100th Day of School.  Can you believe it’s here already?  Several of our primary classrooms have clever activities planned to celebrate this numerical event. To close out the week, classrooms will celebrate friendships with Valentine treats!  After such an extraordinary week, we will be ready for the long Presidents’ Day holiday weekend.  Remember, there is no school for students on Feb. 15th and !6th.

Looking forward to seeing many of you at tomorrow night’s Benefit.  Have a great weekend!

Dr. Welter

The Great Backyard Bird Count: Calling all birdwatchers!  The entire family is invited to join the fun, as we watch for birds during the 13th annual Great Backyard Bird Count (GBBC) coming up this February 12-15th.  This event is sponsored by the National Audubon Society, the Cornell Lab of Ornithology, and Wild Birds Unlimited.  It's a great educational opportunity for you and your family to help out with bird conservation efforts.  Anyone can take part in the GBBC, from novice bird watchers to experts.  You can count the birds you see in your backyard, off your balcony, at your local park, or from your window.  All you have to do is watch for birds for as little as 15 minutes (or longer if you wish) on one or more days of the event, and then submit your sightings online at:  www.birdcount.org  Visit the website for easy-to-follow instructions, cool pictures, and tips for identifying birds.  It's a fun, free and engaging activity that encourages people of all ages to get outside and connect with nature!
