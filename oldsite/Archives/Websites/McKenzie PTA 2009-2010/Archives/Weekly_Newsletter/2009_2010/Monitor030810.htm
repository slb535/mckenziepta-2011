The month of March also brings us a new Habit of Mind—Thinking and Communicating with Clarity and Purpose.  Research tells us that language and thinking are closely entwined. Intelligent people strive to communicate accurately, using precise language, defining terms, and specific descriptions.  They support their statements with explanations, comparisons, and evidence.  Because fuzzy language is a reflection of fuzzy thinking, we encourage our students to expand their word choices, avoiding vague overgeneralizations and distortions. You can support this habit at home by helping your child find new, “crisp” ways of describing objects and events.  Each time you hear words such as “nice,” or “stuff,” try to engage your child in playful ways of substituting precise words.  As our children become word wizards, they will also become critical thinkers!

On March 14th, from 1:00-4:00 pm, District 39 schools, along with St. Joseph, will be participating in Wilmette’s Environmental Fair (GGM).  The Village of Wilmette has partnered with Go Green Wilmette, along with the Wilmette Public Library, the Environmental and Energy Commission, local garden clubs and more.  This community event will provide Environmental Awareness information and sponsor an Electronics Recycling Drive.  The drop-off location is the parking lot of First Presbyterian Church, 600 9th Street. See below for a list of recyclable items.

Before you know it, Spring Break will be here!  Please note there is an early release planned for March 26th with dismissal at 12:15.  For updated calendar information, be sure to check the Google calendar on the district website (www.wilmette39.org).

Wishing you a wonderful weekend!
