Spanish Newsletter!

 Spring is in the air and the students at McKenzie are continuing to reach new heights in Spanish!

Kindergarten: After break, the kindergarten students started their unit on colors.  All classes are studying colors such as rojo (red), azul (blue), and morado (purple).  We have read many books together in order to give the students the opportunity to recognize the colors and repeat them aloud.  We have also played games such as ¿Qué falta? (What’s missing?)  In this game, the students look at a group of color flashcards to try and figure out which one the teacher has taken away.  A great conversation activity would be to have your child tell you what colors they are wearing en español!   

1st Grade:  The first graders have entered one of the most exciting units of the year: Mercado (Market). The students learned about  “bartering” and have had many opportunities to practice negotiating prices as if they were working in a market.  At the end of the month, the students will take part in their own (students-only) Mercado at McKenzie.  In preparation, the students are learning about the crafts that are sold in the markets in México.  They have also begun to make some of these crafts at school.  Ask your students to tell you about the crafts they have made!

2nd Grade: When the second grade students returned for break, they jumped right into their Selva Tropical (Rainforest) unit.  The students have learned about the various animals that live in the Selva and have worked on describing them en español.  In addition, they studied the four levels of the Selva: the Suelo (level 1), Primer Piso (level 2), Canopí (Level 3) y La Capa Emergente (Level 4).  The second graders also solved their very own McKenzie mystery!  They became “professional” detectives as they analyzed Spanish clues left for them by one of the Rainforest animals.  In order to do so, they were expected to ask and answer questions in Spanish…in complete sentences!!  They solved the mystery with flying colors!

3rd Grade:  In third grade, the students have spent time studying Rocas Ígneas (Igneous Rocks).  The third graders looked at some rocks up close and tried to figure out which ones they were.  They did this by observing the appearance and texture of the rocks.  Rocas Ígneas are formed from volcanoes, so it only made sense to go in-depth into the eruption process of a volcano.  We then learned about a group of islands formed by volcanoes.  Las Islas Canarias (The Canary Islands) belong to Spain even though they are off the coast of Africa. The students are singing songs and playing games to help them remember the different islands and where they are located on the map.

4th Grade:  Fourth graders finished up their house unit right before Spring Break with a challenging listening activity on digital voice recorders.  As they listened to the voice on the recorder, they needed to draw the description they heard for each room.  Then students took their version of the house with them as they went on a scavenger hunt to try to find out whose home matched a particular description.  The tricky part was that they had to talk to each other in complete sentences in order to find the correct answers.  Now, students are going out of this world as we begin our study of los planetas en el sistema solar!  Ask your students if they can chant the pneumonic device that was designed to help them remember the order of the planets from the sun in Spanish.
