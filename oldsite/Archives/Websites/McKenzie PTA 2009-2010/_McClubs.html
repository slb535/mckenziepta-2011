Winter Session 2010

Welcome to McClubs!  The purpose of McClubs is to provide an opportunity for our children to explore their individual interests.  We hope to provide a safe, stimulating, and comfortable environment with highly qualified teachers who will guide the children in expanding their knowledge and skills beyond the school curriculum.

Class sizes are limited and are available only to students currently enrolled at McKenzie.  Participation will be determined by a lottery for those classes where interest exceeds the number of openings.  Waiting lists will be established when necessary.  Minimum enrollment is required for some McClubs.  Registration will be closed after classes begin (no exceptions to this).

You will receive an email confirmation with a class list if your child is enrolled in the club.  You will receive a phone call if  you child is on a waiting list or cannot be enrolled in the club.  Please note the start date of your club, as there will not be any future reminders for the start of the McClubs.

REGISTRATION FORMS AND FEES ARE DUE BY 3:00 P.M. FRIDAY, JANUARY 15, 2010.   FEES MUST BE PAID IN FULL FOR EACH MCCLUB AT THE TIME OF REGISTRATION. 

Most McClubs (With the exception of Morning Sports Club) will begin at 3:20 p.m. and end at 4:15 p.m.  Morning Sports Club will begin at 8:00 a.m. and end at 8:45 a.m. Please note the time the club is ending and pick up your child promptly.

McBRIDGE CLUB (GRADES 2, 3 & 4)

Duplicate bridge is now being played at the Olympic Games in the new Mind Games category.  Bill Gates and Warren Buffet believe so much in the value of their favorite pastime that they have donated one million dollars for an initiative to get programs going for kids across the U.S.  Bridge improves logic and memory and teaches teamwork; but the reason children say they love it?  It’s fun!

HOOPS (Grades 1, 2, 3 & 4)

HOOPS is an after-school basketball clinic for boys and girls conducted by One on One, a group of skilled instructors with collegiate experience who have been trained extensively to teach young players the game of basketball.  The instructional sessions combine fundamental skills and organized team competition with the goal of providing an enjoyable and educational after school athletic event.  Developing players benefit by improving their individual skills such as passing, dribbling, shooting, rebounding, and defending while, at the same time, learning how to work within the framework of a team.  All skill levels are encouraged to join.  HOOPS instructors recognize the importance of learning the fundamentals at a young age.  Each instructor believes in the merits of dedication, hard work and positive thinking and is committed to making each of these ideals an integral part of the HOOPS program.
